Orientation

A CA colleague, not a chatbot.

CA Prompt Engineering is a Custom GPT built for an Indian practice. It thinks in AY cycles, TDS quarters, GST closes and MCA filings. The difference from a general chatbot is not tone. It is the source it reasons from.

This assistant
  • Reasons from the bare Acts and Rules loaded into it
  • Flags every rate, threshold and due date to verify
  • Runs 114 ready prompts for you, you write none
A general chatbot
  • Answers from memory that goes stale after each Budget
  • States sections and rates confidently, even when wrong
  • Hands you a blank box and asks you to engineer prompts

An honest limit: grounding in primary law sharply reduces wrong sections and rates. It does not make the tool infallible. Every output stays a draft for your review.

How to use

Four steps to a usable draft.

You never write a prompt. You describe the job, answer a short numbered menu, and collect a finished draft. The whole loop takes under a minute.

The menu is the trick that saves typing. When the assistant asks:

Q1. Client type?

1. Salaried   2. Business   3. NRI 

Q2. Assessment year?

1. AY 2025-26   2. AY 2024-25 
Your reply1, 1

If you give partial detail, the assistant fills the rest with sensible defaults and tells you what it assumed, so you can correct it in one line.

How to ask

Prompt structure, in plain words.

There is no prompt syntax to memorise. The assistant builds the formal structure internally. You only need to keep four plain answers in mind, and the menu pulls most of them out of you anyway.

  • W
    Who is it forSalaried, business, NRI, the company, or yourself.
  • W
    What is the jobA reply, a checklist, a comparison, an engagement letter.
  • P
    Which periodThe AY, FY or quarter, if it matters. If you skip it, AY 2025-26 is assumed.
  • F
    Final formEmail, WhatsApp, file note, or checklist. Ask later if unsure.
Vague, still works
help me with my client's tax notice
Clear, works better
draft a reply to a 143(1) notice, salaried client, AY 2025-26

Either way you land in the same place. A vague line just means the assistant asks one or two more questions before it drafts.

Document intelligence

Paste a notice. It reads it for you.

Drop the text of any notice, letter or order straight into the box. Before doing anything else, the assistant reads it and tells you what it is.

  • A
    Identifies the typeIncome tax section (143(1), 148/148A, 142(1), 245, 139(9), 133(6)) or GST form (DRC-01, ASMT-10, ADT-02, SCN 73/74).
  • B
    Extracts the factsSection or form, demand amount, response deadline, AY or period, and issuing ward where shown.
  • C
    Routes and pre-fillsSends you to the right prompt with the extracted facts already filled in.

Deadline alert: if the response is due within 15 days, it flags the count and recommends immediate action. Section 148 or 148A matters always carry a mandatory partner-review flag.

Reading the report

Every output, the same shape.

A structured CA report always lands in five zones, so you know where to look before you read a word.

  • A
    Firm headerConfirms the output is from your branded assistant, ready for the file.
  • B
    The deliverableThe actual email, checklist, note or table, copy-ready. Client text carries no jargon, internal text uses CA terminology.
  • C
    Assumptions madeEvery default it used, including the AY and FY, so nothing is hidden.
  • D
    Verify before useThe rates, thresholds, due dates and section refs to confirm against official sources. This is your sign-off list.
  • E
    What nextThree or four next actions, including a one-tap QC-1 and the logical next prompt.

The report is a draft, never a filing. You verify the flagged items, apply your judgement, and deliver. The assistant supports the work, it does not sign it.
